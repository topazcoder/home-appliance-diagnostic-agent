REFRIGERATOR REPAIR GUIDE
Appliance Type: Fridge
Source: fridge_repair_manual_v1

--- SYMPTOM: NOT COOLING ---
If your refrigerator is not cooling properly:
1. Check the temperature settings. The fridge compartment should be set between 3°C and 5°C (37°F–41°F). The freezer should be at -18°C (0°F).
2. Ensure the fridge is not overpacked. Overcrowding blocks airflow between compartments.
3. Check that the condenser coils at the back or underneath the fridge are not covered in dust. Vacuum the coils carefully with a brush attachment.
4. Inspect the door seals for gaps or tears. Close the door on a piece of paper — if it slides out easily, the seal needs replacement.
5. Listen for the compressor running (a low hum). If the compressor is silent and the fridge is warm, the compressor or start relay may be faulty.
6. Check if the evaporator fan inside the freezer compartment is running. If not, the fan motor may need replacement.
If the fridge is still not cooling after cleaning coils and checking seals, a technician should inspect the refrigerant level and compressor.

--- SYMPTOM: FREEZING FOOD IN FRIDGE COMPARTMENT ---
If food in the fridge section is freezing:
1. Raise the temperature setting by 1–2 degrees.
2. Check that food is not placed directly in front of the air vents inside the fridge. Move items away from vents.
3. Inspect the temperature sensor (thermistor). A faulty thermistor causes the fridge to over-cool. This requires professional diagnosis.

--- SYMPTOM: WATER LEAKING INSIDE FRIDGE ---
If water is pooling inside the fridge:
1. Locate the defrost drain hole at the back wall inside the fridge compartment. Clear any blockage using a turkey baster with warm water.
2. Check the drain pan underneath the fridge. If it is cracked or overflowing, replace it.
3. Inspect the water dispenser line if your fridge has one. Tighten any loose connections.

--- SYMPTOM: ICE MAKER NOT WORKING ---
If the ice maker is not producing ice:
1. Check that the ice maker is switched on (some models have a wire arm or toggle switch).
2. Ensure the freezer temperature is at or below -12°C (10°F). Ice makers do not work above this temperature.
3. Check the water supply line at the back of the fridge. Make sure the shutoff valve is open and the line is not kinked.
4. Inspect the water inlet valve for blockage or failure. If water does not flow when the valve is energized, the valve needs replacement.
5. Reset the ice maker by pressing and holding the reset button for 10 seconds (location varies by model).

--- SYMPTOM: LOUD NOISE ---
If your refrigerator is making unusual noises:
1. A rattling noise often comes from the drain pan vibrating. Push it firmly back into place.
2. A clicking sound when the compressor tries to start may indicate a faulty start relay. Shake the relay — if it rattles, replace it.
3. A gurgling or hissing sound is normal — it is refrigerant flowing through the system.
4. A loud buzzing from the back is usually the condenser fan hitting debris. Clean the fan blades carefully.
