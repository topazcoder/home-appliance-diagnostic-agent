DRYER REPAIR GUIDE
Appliance Type: Dryer
Source: dryer_repair_manual_v1

--- SYMPTOM: NOT HEATING ---
If your dryer runs but produces no heat:
1. Check the lint filter and clean it completely. A clogged lint filter is the most common cause of heating failure.
2. Check the exhaust vent duct from the back of the dryer to the outside wall. A blocked vent causes the thermal fuse to blow. Clear any lint buildup.
3. For electric dryers: check your home circuit breaker. Electric dryers use two breaker poles — one pole may have tripped while the other is still on, causing the drum to spin but not heat.
4. Test the thermal fuse with a multimeter. The thermal fuse is a one-time safety device located on the exhaust duct inside the dryer. If it has no continuity, replace it.
5. Check the heating element for breaks or burns. A broken heating element requires replacement.
If the thermal fuse keeps blowing after replacement, the root cause is always a blocked vent duct — clear it completely before replacing the fuse again.

--- SYMPTOM: NOT STARTING ---
If your dryer does not start at all:
1. Check that the door is fully closed. The door switch must be engaged for the dryer to start.
2. Check the power supply. Ensure the dryer is plugged in and the circuit breaker has not tripped.
3. Press the Start button firmly and hold for 2–3 seconds. Some models require a held press.
4. Check if a child lock is activated. Refer to your model's manual to deactivate it.
5. Test the door switch with a multimeter for continuity. A faulty door switch prevents starting.

--- SYMPTOM: TAKING TOO LONG TO DRY ---
If clothes take multiple cycles to dry:
1. Clean the lint filter before every cycle. Even a partially clogged filter significantly reduces drying efficiency.
2. Inspect the exhaust vent duct for partial blockage. Disconnect the duct and check for lint buildup throughout its length.
3. Ensure the vent duct is not kinked or crushed behind the dryer.
4. Check the duct length. Ducts longer than 25 feet (with no bends) reduce airflow. Each 90-degree bend reduces effective length by 5 feet.
5. Do not overload the dryer. Clothes need room to tumble freely for efficient drying.
6. If using a gas dryer, check that the gas supply valve is fully open.

--- SYMPTOM: NOISY OPERATION ---
If your dryer makes squealing, thumping, or grinding noises:
1. A squealing noise usually indicates a worn drum support roller or idler pulley. These require replacement.
2. A thumping noise when the drum turns may indicate a worn or broken drum glide (drum slide). Inspect and replace if worn.
3. A grinding noise may indicate a worn drum bearing at the back of the drum. This requires disassembly and professional repair.
4. Check for foreign objects (coins, buttons) inside the drum by running it empty.

--- SYMPTOM: DRUM NOT TURNING ---
If the dryer heats but the drum does not spin:
1. The drive belt is likely broken. The drive belt wraps around the drum and is driven by the motor. Open the dryer cabinet and inspect the belt.
2. If the belt is intact, check the idler pulley and drum rollers for seizure.
3. A seized motor is less common but possible — if the motor hums but does not turn, it may need replacement.
