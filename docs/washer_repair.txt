WASHING MACHINE REPAIR GUIDE
Appliance Type: Washer
Source: washer_repair_manual_v1

--- SYMPTOM: LEAKING WATER ---
If your washing machine is leaking water, follow these steps:
1. Check the door seal (gasket) for cracks, tears, or debris buildup. Wipe the seal with a damp cloth and inspect carefully.
2. Inspect the water inlet hoses at the back of the machine. Tighten any loose connections. Replace hoses that are cracked or bulging.
3. Check the detergent drawer. Overfilling with detergent causes overflow leaks from the front. Use only HE (High Efficiency) detergent in the recommended amount.
4. Inspect the drain hose for cracks or loose connections at the back of the machine or at the standpipe.
5. Run an empty cycle and observe where the water is coming from to isolate the source.
If leaking persists after these steps, the door seal or water pump may need professional replacement.

--- SYMPTOM: NOT SPINNING ---
If your washing machine drum is not spinning:
1. Check if the load is unbalanced. Redistribute clothes evenly inside the drum and restart the spin cycle.
2. Check the lid switch or door latch. The machine will not spin if the door is not fully closed and latched.
3. Inspect the drain filter (usually behind a small panel at the front bottom). A clogged filter prevents the machine from draining and spinning. Remove debris and rinse the filter.
4. Check if the machine is in a paused or delayed start mode. Cancel the cycle and restart.
5. Listen for a humming sound without spinning — this may indicate a worn drive belt or faulty motor coupling.
If the drum still does not spin after clearing the filter and rebalancing the load, the drive belt or motor coupling likely needs replacement by a technician.

--- SYMPTOM: NOISY DURING CYCLE ---
If your washing machine makes loud banging, grinding, or rattling noises:
1. Check pockets for coins, keys, or small objects that may have entered the drum.
2. Ensure the machine is level on the floor. Adjust the leveling feet at the base until the machine does not rock.
3. Check if the load is too large. Overloading causes the drum to bang against the casing. Reduce load size.
4. A grinding noise during spin may indicate worn drum bearings. This requires professional repair.
5. A rattling noise may indicate loose counterweights inside the machine. Do not attempt to fix this yourself.

--- SYMPTOM: NOT DRAINING ---
If your washing machine is not draining water after a cycle:
1. Check and clean the drain filter located at the front bottom panel. This is the most common cause.
2. Inspect the drain hose for kinks or blockages. Straighten the hose and ensure it is not inserted too far into the standpipe.
3. Run a spin-only cycle to force drainage.
4. Check if the pump is running. If you hear no pump sound, the pump may be faulty and needs replacement.

--- SYMPTOM: FOUL SMELL ---
If your washing machine has a bad odor:
1. Run a hot wash cycle (60°C or higher) with a washing machine cleaner or two cups of white vinegar and no laundry.
2. Clean the door seal thoroughly, paying attention to the folds where mold accumulates.
3. Leave the door open after every wash to allow the drum to dry out.
4. Clean the detergent drawer monthly by removing it and rinsing under hot water.
